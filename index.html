Phishing Awareness Tips

If you have any concerns about the legitimacy of this phishing exercise, please reach out to the IT department.

Phishing emails can be tricky to identify. Here are some helpful hints:

  1. Check the Email Address: Sometimes, the sender appears familiar, but the email address may be incorrect (e.g., dicklovett-co.uk instead of dicklovett.co.uk).
  2. Assess Urgency: Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency to prompt quick action without considering the message's legitimacy.
  3. Inspect Links: Hover over links to reveal their destinations without clicking. Similar to email addresses, fake web addresses may resemble genuine sites.

What to Do If You're Unsure:

  • Verify the Source: Contact the sender directly to confirm the email's legitimacy.
  • Avoid Clicking Untrusted Links: If you followed a link or entered credentials, inform the IT department promptly for support.

Remember, no one is immune to phishing emails. Stay vigilant and seek assistance if needed.
